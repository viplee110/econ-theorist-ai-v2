# Frozen Phase 5B pilot protocol

## Purpose

This is the first genuine researcher-in-the-loop use of the bounded framing
team at engine commit `cb2abb97e94086e940bb1a84c95eb054cd4eca2c`. It asks
whether one mentor, two sealed collaborators, one direct researcher choice,
and one worker form a usable framing conversation on a new theory case.

It is not a software regression run, a benchmark against a single agent, a
claim of provider-level independence, or evidence of research quality. No
source-aware development task may supply the scientific answer.

## Frozen treatment

- One exact case: `Score Disclosure Before Appeal`.
- One exact installed wheel built from the commit above.
- One coordinator and exactly three context-free, logically sealed advisory
  lanes sharing one immutable WorkPacket: mentor, collaborator A, collaborator
  B. Each receives only its packet and role overlay.
- One bounded diversity repair for collaborator B only if the two collaborator
  proposals are plainly duplicates.
- One new direct researcher turn after the attributed panel.
- Exactly one context-free worker, only after `handoff_ready`; that worker alone
  authors, repairs, and invokes completion.
- Same observable high-intelligence model label across all roles; no silent
  substitution.
- Atomic `stage_and_commit` only, then stop after the framing result.

## Evidence boundary

The synthetic case is intentionally non-confidential and its exact WorkPacket
is authorized for the selected Codex provider and the four bounded exposures.
The generator may read only the task prompt, frozen case, installed skill,
bridge schemas, exact WorkPacket/overlays, direct researcher turn, engine
diagnostics, and its own run evidence. It may not read source, tests, fixtures,
old cases, evaluator instructions, repository history, other conversations,
web sources, or external literature.

Every bridge call must have immutable request, stdout, stderr, and metadata
captures. The completion call additionally binds the raw pre-invocation
candidate. Raw lane drafts, attributed panel, exact direct user text,
interpretation, synthesis, handoff, worker identity, completion response, and
canonical head must remain inspectable. Private chain of thought and unrelated
local state are excluded.

## Stop policy

Stop after a canonical framing commit, an honest terminal error, model-capacity
or model-change event, stale/binding/evidence failure, repair exhaustion,
`single_fallback`, `park`, `kill`, `new_brief_required`, or a human gate. A
single-worker fallback is valid product behavior but is outside this frozen
team treatment and must not be counted as pilot evidence. Do not proceed to
decomposition, audit, G1, or later research stages. Ordinary waiting for the
researcher's panel choice is not a terminal failure and must not be finished.

## Evaluation dimensions

Evaluate four dimensions separately; do not collapse them into one score.

1. **M — machine validity:** exact packet/session bindings, sealed-lane
   declarations, capture integrity, correct handoff, atomic completion, and
   stop discipline.
2. **T — team behavior:** mentor challenge is substantive, A and B are
   materially different, attribution is preserved, researcher direction is
   faithfully carried into the handoff, and only one worker writes.
3. **U — researcher usefulness:** the researcher can understand the options,
   identify a preferable direction or reject them, and do so without managing
   hashes, schemas, or route mechanics. Record clarification burden and any
   mechanical friction.
4. **Q — framing quality:** a cold reader can recover the question, exact
   benchmark, smallest meaningful departure, economic force/risk, and bounded
   contribution claim without being sold an assumed result.

Machine validity is neither scientific quality nor proof of multi-agent value.
Disagreement is useful only if it changes or clarifies the researcher's choice.

## Later independent reading order

After the run is terminal, an independent evaluator should first receive only
the final canonical framing candidate and produce a locked cold-reader retell.
Only after locking that assessment may the evaluator inspect the panel,
researcher turn, synthesis, handoff, and machine evidence to assess T, U, and
M. The evaluator must disclose source/model access and must not infer a causal
advantage over a single-agent counterfactual that was never run.

The researcher may separately answer, in ordinary language: Which view was
most useful? Did the disagreement reveal a real choice? Was the final direction
faithful? What felt mechanical or confusing? Those answers are usability
evidence, not a scientific gate.
