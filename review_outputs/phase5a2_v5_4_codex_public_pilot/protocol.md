## 1. Purpose and claim boundary

This is a fresh, bounded regression pilot of the research-first repair driven
by the failed V5.3 public run. It uses the same public case, generator input,
model family, budgets, isolation, retry allowance, and primary V5.3
classification gate. The only generator-facing and runtime treatment is the
frozen engine package containing R7/N6, exact runtime-hash materialization,
distinctive-mechanism binding, consequence and public-state binding, and the
economist-facing memo projection.

V5.4 also freezes a richer E1-E6 diagnostic key before generation. That key is
secondary analysis, not the cross-run scoring line. It cannot retrospectively
reclassify V5.3 or support a V5.3/V5.4 causal quality claim.

The pilot asks:

1. whether runtime-derived relation metadata is removed from the language
   model's task and materialized before canonical candidate identity;
2. whether a zero-certificate payoff witness can no longer validate a
   certificate-depletion claim;
3. whether a focal stock/depletion mechanism can no longer reuse the same
   path as its contrast while claiming distinctiveness; and
4. whether the system behaves like an economic collaborator by either
   supporting a coherent branch or clearly routing an unsupported object to
   revision.

This pilot cannot establish that V2 is generally better than V1, lowers human
effort in other projects, produces Top-5 work, or is ready for submission.
Those are comparative Phase 6 claims.

## 4. Execution path and retry discipline

The installed engine, not this protocol or the generator, selects every route.
The expected pre-G1 path remains:

1. frame.question_and_benchmarks;
2. decompose.primitives;
3. audit.framing_economics; and then
4. a human-owned G1 wait or an engine-owned framing revision.

The generator completes one authorized route at a time and continues only
after a canonical committed response. It stops before G1 and never fabricates
or infers a human decision.

One candidate attempt means writing a new candidate and issuing one complete
request. Resume and exact replay do not consume an attempt. Runtime hash
materialization must not consume a repair attempt. Before every complete call,
the raw candidate bytes are copied to a unique attempt file and hashed.

After a first or second repairable candidate-validation diagnostic, the
generator must repair the same immutable route. It may not call finish for a
normal validation error, waiting, clarification, handoff, or intentional
pause. Finish is allowed only after all three candidates fail, explicit
cancellation, or a genuine host/model terminal condition, and its reason must
match the observed event.

Stale base, conflict, unexpected route selection, isolation failure, or
request/response binding failure is a non-candidate protocol diagnostic: it
stops the pilot without forcing another route and without recording retry
exhaustion or finish. If the audit commits, one ordinary continuation is
captured:

- a human G1 wait stops the pilot without a Decision;
- an engine-selected framing revision is retained as the next packet and the
  pilot intentionally pauses without finish; and
- another agent route must follow the engine selection without replaying the
  initial framing inputs.

After generation freezes, the final successful complete request, or a
legitimate terminal finish request, is replayed with exact request bytes.
Response bytes, candidate identity, canonical head, and receipt must be
identical. If the terminal request is the successful runtime-null audit
complete, that replay supplies additional live materialization evidence. The
pilot does not alter generator input, add an external mid-run controller, or
force a nonterminal audit request to be replayable after its route state has
changed.

## 6. Directed repair regression

This directed result is reported separately from research quality.

### Runtime materialization

Pass requires:

- the original R7 draft uses JSON null at the unique runtime-bound relation;
- the bridge emits no runtime-hash repair diagnostic;
- the source draft is not rewritten;
- the canonical relation contains the independently recomputed hash of the
  complete current FramingQualityBundle economic-interpretation facet; and
- post-freeze deterministic rematerialization of the same frozen draft and
  exact contract preserves the materialized bytes and candidate digest.

Repeated-completion behavior is already covered by the frozen focused
regression. If the successful audit complete is also the terminal replay, the
pilot records that additional live evidence; otherwise its absence is not a
materialization failure and no new controller instruction is introduced.

If the generator writes its own explicit hash, the candidate may be valid, but
this run has not exercised the intended repair and the directed check is
inconclusive.

### Scientific safeguard

The system must not canonically accept:

- identical focal and contrast paths as a distinctive mechanism;
- a zero-stock condition as evidence of a decreasing depletion transition;
- a choice-mediated claim without the same consequence, same public state,
  and complete feasible-action comparison; or
- a mechanical transition that hides an operative choice.

The generator need not deliberately submit a bad candidate. An initially
correct object, an honest not_claimed or unresolved status, or a precise
revise-framing disposition can satisfy the safeguard.

## 7. Four independent outcome dimensions

The final report records four non-substitutable outcomes:

- M: machine execution and protocol;
- A: quality of the system's economic audit;
- O: current research-object status, READY, REVISE, or KILL;
- R/H: reader transfer and remaining human burden.

A correct result may be M-PASS / A-SUCCESS / O-REVISE. That means the research
system successfully found and explained a model defect; it does not mean that
the research object is ready for G1.

Canonical R7 acceptance means that structured declarations passed the machine
contract. It does not prove payoff algebra, state reachability, a nonempty
parameter region, a natural buyer tradeoff, a true stock-composition effect,
or readable intuition.

### A/O: economics and research-object gate

Fluent prose cannot compensate for a critical semantic error. The independent
evaluator applies the frozen key to the upstream ledger actually chosen by the
fresh generator rather than mechanically imposing the V5.3 answer.

The audit must:

- define what certification guarantees and compare the complete feasible
  buyer action envelope in the relevant positive-stock and zero-stock states;
- distinguish buyer-choice-mediated from genuinely mechanical depletion;
- connect issuance, stock/depletion, conditional pool composition, inspection
  return or choice, and the target outcome with a real state transition or
  equivalent stock law;
- distinguish the focal model from each contrast without calling a broader
  institutional deletion a pure depletion effect;
- keep pointwise policy from being confused with an aggregate, and a fixed
  selector from being confused with selection robustness; and
- state a falsifiable kill condition and downgrade or revise every unresolved
  central link.

A new price, matching value, or buyer-facing tradeoff is admissible only if it
was introduced honestly upstream and its payoff region is supported. The audit
may not add one sentence of new primitives to rescue the mechanism.

### R/H: reader-transfer and human-burden gate

Using only the economist-facing memo or dossier, a cold theory reader must
recover either:

- a closed mechanism and its benchmark differences, if the system recommends
  readiness; or
- the exact failed link, why it fails, and the high-value human choice needed
  next, if the system recommends revision.

Internal route, validator, schema, node-id, active-margin, or G1 jargon does
not count as economic intuition. Human burden is reported from H0 (confirmation
only) to H4 (the researcher must rediscover the central contradiction and
rebuild the model).

## 9. Decision matrix

- M-PASS / A-SUCCESS / O-READY / R-PASS: this case's framing passes.
- M-PASS / A-SUCCESS / O-REVISE / R-PASS: the system succeeds as a research
  collaborator, while the object requires a structural human choice.
- M-PASS / A-FAIL: the dangerous case in which machinery works but economic
  reasoning remains wrong or falsely recommends readiness.
- M-FAIL: protocol or engine failure; scientific content is diagnostic only.

The primary V5.3 summary labels remain:

- Pass when machine, economics, and reader-transfer gates pass;
- Scientific revise when machinery works but economics or reader transfer
  fails; and
- Protocol/engine fail when isolation, authority, binding, canonical state,
  retry, finish, or replay fails.

Only these primary labels use the cross-run scoring line. The four-dimensional
matrix and E1-E6 key are pre-generation secondary diagnostics intended to
explain research usefulness, not to move the V5.3 threshold.

One fresh stochastic run is evidence about recurrence of the old failure under
this frozen treatment. It is not a general causal or Phase 6 quality claim.
