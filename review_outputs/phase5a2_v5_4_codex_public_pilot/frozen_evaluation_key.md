## 2. Admissible economic branches

The seed does not force one certificate interpretation. Evaluate the ledger
the fresh run actually commits.

- Guaranteed-service branch: if a visible certificate guarantees successful
  service at fixed buyer terms, buying it gives value one. Costly inspection
  yields at most one minus c. Inspection is strictly dominated whenever that
  certificate is available. A zero-certificate inspection witness cannot show
  certificate depletion.
- Information-only branch: if certification is explicitly a persistent signal
  about past or current capacity rather than a guaranteed service unit, the
  certified outside option must be rederived from that ledger. Do not import
  the guaranteed-service inequality mechanically.
- Genuine buyer-tradeoff branch: price, match value, congestion, rationing, or
  another buyer-facing force may activate inspection in a positive-stock
  state only if it appears upstream, has the same timing and information, and
  admits a supported nonempty payoff region.
- Mechanical-depletion branch: depletion can be technological after trade,
  but positive-stock trade must itself be supported. A hidden buyer choice
  cannot be relabeled mechanical.
- Honest-revision branch: identifying an unresolved primitive, state law, or
  dominance problem and routing it upstream is a successful audit even though
  the research object is not ready.

The economics audit may not introduce a new primitive in prose to save the
upstream mechanism.

## 3. Economics rubric

Score each item 2 (supported), 1 (identified but unresolved), or 0 (missed,
incorrect, or overstated).

E1-E5 are the critical economics items. E6 is a secondary intuition and
reader-transfer item. This partition is frozen before generation.

### E1. Certificate semantics and complete payoff envelope

The object says exactly what a certificate guarantees. In zero-, one-, and
two-certificate public states, it compares every feasible relevant action:
certified purchase, inspection, uninspected purchase, and exit. It respects
the chosen timing, beliefs, continuation, and trade terms.

### E2. Nature of depletion

The object distinguishes buyer-choice-mediated from mechanical depletion.
Inspection activity in a no-certificate state is not evidence of depletion.
Mechanical depletion in a positive-stock state cannot hide an unsupported
trade choice.

### E3. Real stock/depletion spine

The focal argument actually connects:

    k -> issuance -> stock/depletion transition -> conditional uncertified
    composition -> inspection return or choice -> target outcome

An explicit stock balance, transition law, or equivalent state accounting is
required at framing precision. Merely adding stock node names or saying that
depletion may change beliefs is insufficient.

### E4. Benchmark meaning

F and its contrast use genuinely different nodes or transitions. If a
benchmark deletes persistence and depletion together, it is described as a
broader institutional boundary rather than a pure depletion effect. A fixed
pointwise inspection rule is not a fixed aggregate inspection rate. A fixed
selector is a convention, not selection robustness.

### E5. Falsification and disposition

Kill conditions include the relevant possibilities: stock cannot persist,
depletion does not change conditional pool composition, a matched contrast
reproduces the same mechanism, or the required buyer margin has no admissible
region. A broad parameter search alone does not establish readiness.
Unresolved central links imply revision or killing, not ready_for_g1.

### E6. Economic intuition and institutional naturalness

A theory economist can trace one certificate's economic life and explain how
k changes issuance, stock states, pool composition, and search incentives.
The output separates established mechanism, illustration, and conjecture and
explains what institution the certificate represents.

## 4. Outcome rule

- O-READY requires E1-E5 all equal to 2. E6 recovery is reported separately
  under R; an exposition failure can therefore produce O-READY / R-FAIL.
- O-REVISE applies when one or more of E1-E5 equals 1, none equals 0, and the
  system accurately discloses each defect and names the upstream repair or
  human structural choice. It also applies when the current branch is
  contradicted but a credible upstream repair or alternative branch remains
  within the intended question.
- O-KILL applies only when the central intended mechanism is contradicted and
  no credible admissible repair remains within the intended question.
- A-FAIL applies when any of E1-E5 equals 0, when any score of 1 is not
  accurately disclosed and correctly disposed, or when an unclosed mechanism
  is presented as ready.

A-SUCCESS requires no E1-E5 score of 0 and either:

- all E1-E5 equal 2 with no false readiness claim; or
- every score of 1 is accurately disclosed and the system selects O-REVISE or
  O-KILL rather than readiness.

Correct mechanical depletion, a genuinely supported buyer tradeoff, and an
honest revise disposition can each be A-SUCCESS.

## 5. Cold-reader probes

The reader first gives an unconstrained retell, then answers:

1. What exactly does the certificate guarantee?
2. With one visible certificate, what is the buyer's complete action set and
   payoff comparison?
3. Why may search occur with no certificate, and can depletion occur then?
4. Which exact state or transition is present in F but absent from N?
5. Why can aggregate inspection change under a fixed pointwise rule?
6. What observation or derivation kills the mechanism?
7. In at most 150 words, explain the core intuition to another theorist.

If the object is READY, the reader must recover a closed mechanism. If it is
REVISE, the reader must recover the failed link and the next high-value choice;
the reader is not expected to invent a missing model.

## 6. Human burden

- H0: confirmation only.
- H1: choose among clearly developed research branches.
- H2: one local derivation or structural rewrite remains.
- H3: several mechanism links or the narrative must be rebuilt.
- H4: the researcher must discover the central contradiction and remodel.

The target is that a human can make a structural G1 decision without first
rediscovering a hidden economic contradiction. A clearly developed choice
between mechanical depletion and a genuine buyer tradeoff is H1. Asking the
human to discover dominance, supply the stock law, and rewrite all benchmarks
is H3-H4.

## 7. Machine versus economics

R7 can check declared consequence/state bindings, path order, zero-stock
depletion misuse, formal focal/contrast differences, and hidden choice nodes.
It cannot prove payoff algebra, state reachability, nonempty parameter regions,
institutional naturalness, true composition effects, or reader comprehension.

Therefore canonical R7 acceptance is never itself an economics-pass
certificate.
