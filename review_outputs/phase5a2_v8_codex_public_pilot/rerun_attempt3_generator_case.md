## Researcher-authorized branch

Use the guaranteed-service certificate ledger. Each long-lived seller faces a
sequence of service opportunities. A seller with a current high opportunity
may pay cost `k` for a truthful public certificate that identifies that
particular opportunity. The certified high opportunity remains available and
high while carried. If a buyer trades with it, service succeeds, the buyer
receives normalized value one under common terms, and both the opportunity and
certificate are consumed.

An uncertified current opportunity may be high or low. Before choosing, a
short-lived buyer may pay search cost `c` to inspect one uncertified
opportunity, buy a certified or uncertified opportunity without that
inspection, or exit. Sellers earn a margin from successful trade and discount
future profits. The frame must propose explicitly when fresh opportunities are
drawn, whether and when uncertified opportunities change, and when
recertification can occur. Those timing and transition objects are not already
approved merely because the guaranteed-service branch was selected.

With a selectable visible certificate, do not posit an active inspection
response solely from the composition of the uncertified pool. For `c > 0`,
inspection yields at most `1-c` while certified purchase yields one, so that
inspection margin is dominated; at `c = 0`, state any required tie-breaking
rather than asserting a strict response. Do not add price, match, congestion,
rationing, or another buyer-facing force.
