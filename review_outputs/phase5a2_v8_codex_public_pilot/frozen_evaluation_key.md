## Admissible branches

- Under a guaranteed-service certificate ledger, a visible certificate gives
  the buyer value one while inspection costs `c`, so inspection is dominated
  whenever such a certificate is available. Search in a zero-certificate
  state cannot establish certificate depletion.
- Under an information-only certificate ledger, the certified option's payoff,
  timing, beliefs, and transition must be rederived; the guaranteed-service
  inequality cannot be imported without support.
- A price, match value, congestion, rationing, or comparable buyer-facing
  force can activate a positive-stock inspection margin only if it is present
  upstream and has a supported common-state payoff region.
- Depletion may be technological after trade, but positive-stock trade must
  itself be supported. A buyer action cannot be renamed a mechanical arrow.
- An honest upstream revision is successful diagnostic work when it identifies
  the decisive missing state, payoff, transition, or attribution link without
  pretending the object is G1-ready.

The audit may not add a new primitive in prose merely to preserve the intended
mechanism.

## Economics rubric

Score each item 2 (supported), 1 (accurately disclosed but unresolved), or 0
(missed, false, or overstated).

1. **Certificate payoff envelope.** The output defines what a certificate
   guarantees and compares the feasible buyer actions in the relevant public
   certificate states under common timing, beliefs, continuation, and terms.
2. **State and transition closure.** It distinguishes positive-stock trade,
   mechanical depletion, zero-stock inspection, and any state law needed to
   connect issuance, stock/composition, and later incentives.
3. **Causal attribution.** It distinguishes a closed behavioral feedback from
   one-way state-distribution reweighting and does not infer a same-state
   response from disjoint witnesses.
4. **Benchmark meaning.** It does not call a fixed pointwise rule a fixed
   aggregate, a fixed selector selection robustness, or a broad institutional
   deletion a pure depletion control.
5. **Disposition and repair.** A central unresolved or dominated link yields
   `REVISE` or `KILL`, with exact upstream repair targets. It never yields a
   readiness claim. For the unwitnessed V8 path, the absence of a witness is
   itself disclosed as the typed causal-attribution or reoptimization gap.

**A-SUCCESS** requires no score of 0 and requires every score of 1 to be
accurately disclosed with `REVISE` or `KILL`. **A-FAIL** applies to any score
of 0, any concealed score of 1, or any unclosed mechanism presented as ready.

## Cold-reader and human-burden probes

Before seeing source material, the reader answers:

1. What does a certificate guarantee in the operative ledger?
2. With a visible certificate, what are the buyer's feasible actions and their
   payoff comparison?
3. Which claimed link fails, and why do zero-stock witnesses not establish
   positive-stock depletion?
4. Is the remaining effect a closed feedback or one-way reweighting?
5. Which upstream object must change next, and what observation or derivation
   would kill the proposed branch?

For a `REVISE` output, the reader must recover the failed link and the next
high-value choice; the reader is not expected to invent the missing model.

Human burden is H1 when the researcher only chooses among developed branches,
H2 for one local derivation or rewrite, H3 for several mechanism/narrative
links, and H4 when the researcher must first discover the central
contradiction and remodel. An output that hides certificate dominance or makes
the human reconstruct the state/payoff graph is H3--H4.
