## 1. Purpose and claim boundary

This is a fresh, bounded regression pilot of the V5.3 repair driven by the
failed V5.2 public run. It asks whether the installed engine can traverse the
same pre-G1 framing path while:

1. projecting the exact hard relation topology the candidate must instantiate;
2. testing whether each claimed choice margin is economically active before
   accepting its mechanism prose; and
3. retaining complete, request-bound host evidence without confusing an
   ordinary pause with a terminal failure.

The pilot may establish machine-path correctness and reveal scientific or
reader-facing weaknesses for this one case. It cannot establish that v2 is
better than v1, lowers human effort generally, produces Top-5 work, or is ready
for submission. Those remain Phase 6 claims.

The V5, V5.1, and V5.2 outputs, repository source, tests, reference candidates,
and all post-freeze analysis are inaccessible to the generator until every
V5.3 candidate and bridge response is frozen.

## 6. Noncompensatory acceptance criteria

### Machine gate

- The engine commits each scientifically valid selected route or honestly
  terminates/routes to revision when a scientific gate fails.
- The exact path reaches the economics audit without replaying framing or
  decomposition and resolves default budgets to 4,000, 8,000, and 18,000.
- The audit WorkPacket exposes four exact hard input-to-bundle `audits`
  templates and one exact hard bundle-to-dossier `governs` template, including
  owner facets and semantic-hash bindings.
- No human-owned decision is manufactured.
- Completion, retry, finish, resume, and replay preserve exactly-once canonical
  semantics. A finish receipt does not rewrite or abandon the immutable run.
- Every raw response is untruncated and bound to the exact request operation
  and canonical request digest.

### Economics gate

Fluent prose cannot compensate for a critical semantic error. The audit must
detect, repair, or block:

1. a fixed conditional inspection rule being mistaken for fixed aggregate
   inspection under endogenous composition;
2. a placebo or mechanically different benchmark being called a clean
   consumable-stock intervention;
3. a fixed selector being presented as equilibrium-selection robustness; or
4. a claimed choice-mediated mechanism whose action is dominated, inactive, or
   unresponsive to the upstream primitive in the stated payoff ledger.

Every operative choice force must supply one concrete state, the feasible
action comparison, continuation-inclusive payoff basis, the appropriate binary
exhaustiveness or best-deviation/envelope argument, and an explanation of how
the upstream change moves the payoff gap. An inactive or unresolved margin must
downgrade the attribution and cannot support `ready_for_g1`.

For the frozen seed in particular, the audit must confront that buying from an
available certificate yields service value one while inspection costs
`c in (0,1)` and yields service value at most one unless an additional
buyer-facing tradeoff is explicitly introduced. It must either repair that
primitive ledger or delete/downgrade the buyer-controlled depletion channel.

### Reader-transfer gate

A cold theory reader, using only the economist-facing memo or dossier, must be
able to recover the puzzle, relevant forces, a closed three-link mechanism, a
concrete state/action/payoff comparison, a falsifiable kill condition, and the
role of every benchmark. The memo must avoid internal workflow jargon and list
concrete remaining edits.

## 7. Decision rule

- **Pass:** machine, economics, and reader-transfer gates pass.
- **Scientific revise:** machine behavior is correct but economics or reader
  transfer fails; make only a failure-driven scientific change.
- **Protocol/engine fail:** isolation, request/response binding, route ownership,
  canonical commit, budgeting, exact replay, or terminal evidence fails; repair
  before interpreting prose.
